Roadmap to Economic Justice

April 2022

A strategy to empower, develop, and transform the City of St. Louis through a vibrant, just and growing economy in which all people can thrive.

Download Report (PDF)

Executive Summary

The Roadmap to Economic Justice presents a comprehensive strategy for leveraging American Rescue Plan Act (ARPA) funds to address historic inequities and transform marginalized communities in St. Louis. Developed by the St. Louis Development Corporation (SLDC) and the Office of Mayor Tishaura O. Jones, this document outlines a $246 million investment framework focused on three strategic pillars.

Five Primary Goals

  • Strengthen neighborhoods
  • Eliminate the wealth gap
  • Improve health & educational outcomes
  • Expand the tax base
  • Grow the City's population

Three Strategic Pillars

EMPOWER

$61M

Business empowerment, workforce development, and educational justice initiatives

DEVELOP

$77M

Incentive reform, site assembly & development, and commercial corridor revitalization

TRANSFORM

$108M

Neighborhood capacity building, vacancy & environmental justice, and housing accessibility

Key Initiatives

Economic Empowerment

  • Establish economic empowerment centers in Qualified Census Tracts (QCTs)
  • Create revolving loan funds for small, minority, and woman-owned businesses
  • Build year-round youth-to-jobs programs
  • Develop College Promise program for graduating seniors

Equitable & Inclusive Development

  • Implement transparent economic incentive policies with community benefits
  • Proactively assemble and market development sites
  • Develop MLK corridor as innovation and business district
  • Eradicate digital divide through fiber infrastructure

Neighborhood Transformation

  • Hire neighborhood managers to support community planning
  • Modernize Land Reutilization Authority operations
  • Establish greenlining fund for down-payment assistance
  • Fund anti-displacement efforts for long-term residents

Focus on Qualified Census Tracts

The roadmap prioritizes investments in QCTsâ€”areas with incomes below 60% of Area Median Gross Income or poverty rates above 25%. In St. Louis, 59.2% of the city's geography and 52.9% of residents live in QCTs, making this focus essential for citywide economic growth.

Implementation Strategy

The Roadmap emphasizes the need for partnerships across financial institutions, non-profit anchors, corporations, and foundations to leverage and multiply public investments. It establishes a framework for transparent, community-driven development that addresses root causes of inequality while creating sustainable economic growth.
